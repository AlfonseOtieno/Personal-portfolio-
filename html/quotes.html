Words That Shaped My Thinking

These are not motivational posters. They are ideas from thinkers I respect that changed how I understand work, learning, and discipline.

Consistency
“

When nothing seems to help, I go and look at a stonecutter hammering away at his rock perhaps a hundred times without as much as a crack showing in it. Yet at the hundred and first blow it will split in two, and I know it was not that blow that did it, but all that had gone before.

Jacob Riis

Hard Work
“

The harder I work, the luckier I become.

Gary Player

Discipline
“

We are what we repeatedly do. Excellence, then, is not an act, but a habit.

Aristotle

Systems
“

You do not rise to the level of your goals. You fall to the level of your systems.

James Clear

Focus
“

Clarity about what matters provides clarity about what does not.

Cal Newport

The Brain
“

The brain is not a fixed organ. It is shaped by what you repeatedly do, think, and practice.

Norman Doidge

Execution
“

The most effective way to do it, is to do it.

Amelia Earhart

Focus
“

It is not enough to be busy. The question is: what are we busy about?

Henry David Thoreau

Learning
“

An investment in knowledge pays the best interest.

Benjamin Franklin

Discipline
“

Discipline is choosing between what you want now and what you want most.

Abraham Lincoln
