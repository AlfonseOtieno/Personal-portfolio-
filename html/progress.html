Books Read

Completed: January 2025

The Brain That Changes Itself

Norman Doidge explores the science of neuroplasticity — how the brain can rewire itself through experience and deliberate practice.

  • The brain is not fixed — it reshapes itself based on what you repeatedly do.
  • Deliberate, focused practice drives structural change in the brain.
  • Habits carved through repetition become increasingly automatic over time.
What I applied: Used this to commit to daily coding sessions, treating skill-building as a physical process of brain change, not just willpower.
Completed: February 2025

Atomic Habits

James Clear's framework for building systems of small habits that compound into significant long-term results.

  • Identity-driven habits outlast outcome-driven ones — become the person, not just the goal.
  • Make habits obvious, attractive, easy, and satisfying to reinforce them.
  • 1% improvements are invisible daily but transformative over a year.
What I applied: Rebuilt daily routines around identity — "I am a developer" rather than "I want to learn to code."
Completed: March 2025

Deep Work

Cal Newport makes the case for distraction-free, concentrated work as the defining skill of the knowledge economy.

  • Depth of focus, not quantity of hours, produces the most valuable work.
  • Shallow work expands to fill time if you don't protect your schedule deliberately.
  • Embrace boredom — the ability to focus is trained, not innate.
What I applied: Blocked 2–3 hour deep work sessions for coding and writing, removed phone during those windows.
